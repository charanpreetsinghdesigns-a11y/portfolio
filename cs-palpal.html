The Problem

Screen addiction is a growing crisis

Kids today are spending more time on screens than ever — and parents often don't know how to respond. Blocking technology isn't realistic. What matters is guiding children to use it well.

This assignment explored how design could shift parental control from restriction to guidance — using AI to surface real behavioural insights and connect parents with expert mentors.

The Solution

Meet PalPal

A platform that gives personalised guidance to parents based on their child's behaviour and psychology — helping make screen time positive, not just limited.

Differentiation

Why not other parental control apps?

Most apps block or restrict. PalPal takes a fundamentally different approach — one grounded in child psychology and parent support.

1
Real Guidance, Not Restrictions

Instead of hard limits, PalPal coaches parents on how to redirect screen time into positive behaviour through personalised daily advice.

2
Personalised Parenting Support

AI analyses each child's individual behaviour — tone, mood, interests, usage patterns — to give advice that actually fits your family.

3
Building the Parent-Child Bond

The goal isn't to monitor — it's to improve the relationship. Insights are framed as conversation starters, not surveillance reports.

How It Works

AI that understands your child

A three-step AI loop collects, analyses, and translates child behaviour into actionable guidance for parents.

01

AI converses with the child and analyses tone, interests, and mood.

02

Tracks screen patterns, app usage, and content consumed.

03

Generates behavioural insights and recommendations for parents.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

01

Framing matters more than features. Parents don't want surveillance — they want support. Calling it "guidance" rather than "control" changed every design decision, from copy to colour.

02

Data needs human context. Screen time stats alone create anxiety. Pairing usage data with expert mentors and actionable advice transforms raw numbers into something parents can actually act on.

03

The child is a stakeholder too. Designing for parents means accounting for how the child experiences the app's effects — PalPal needed to feel fair to both sides of the parent-child relationship.
