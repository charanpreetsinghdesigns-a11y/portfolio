The Brief

Integrate AI into your creative workflow

The goal was to explore how AI tools can assist each phase of the UX design process — not just wireframing, but research, brainstorming, and final UI generation too.

I chose to design a German Digital Document Wallet — an app to store and access verified digital copies of bureaucratic documents — as the subject for this end-to-end AI-assisted workflow.

Problem

Germany still relies heavily on paperwork for bureaucracy. Documents are hard to track, store, and retrieve when needed — creating stress and missed deadlines.

Goal

Design an app that stores and organises verified digital copies of important documents. Faster access, less stress, fewer missed deadlines.

Phase 01 — Research

What users want from a unified platform

Used ChatGPT for conversational research and Consensus to search 250M+ peer-reviewed papers for evidence-backed insights.

Save time

Reduce office visits and eliminate manual effort even in supposedly "digital" processes.

One place

24/7 access to all key documents without scattering them across folders, email, and cloud.

Trust & transparency

Lower printing costs, better clarity on document status, and privacy-first consent flows.

Inspiration: DigiLocker (India)

India's government-backed digital document wallet proves the "one app for documents" model works at scale — reducing paperwork and repeated document submissions. The challenge for Germany is structural: decentralised departments, different IT systems, and strict privacy rules mean there's no single shared document infrastructure.

Not a tech problem — it's about aligning government departments, rules, and systems.

Phase 02 — Brainstorming

AI as a thinking partner

Used IdeaMap.AI — a visual workspace for collaborative brainstorming where AI boosts creativity. Useful as a starting point but text-heavy and couldn't support follow-up questions.

Project Assumptions

To move the design forward, I defined these assumptions upfront:

Technology exists to securely store, encrypt, and retrieve verified documents

All government departments are aligned via shared standards/APIs

A single identity login (eID/BundID) is available for authentication

Legal permissions + consent flows allow document sharing across authorities

Users have smartphones + digital literacy to adopt the solution

Phase 04 — UI Generation

Testing AI as a design tool

I tested three AI tools for UI generation, each with a detailed prompt describing the IA, design system (Airbnb-inspired, minimal), and trust requirements.

UI Generation Limited
Figma Make

Generated a starting UI from a single prompt — but was restricted to one prompt only. No iteration or editing possible. Good for a quick visual reference, not for a real design workflow.

UI Generation Inconsistent
Google Stitch

Allowed unlimited improvements but screens weren't consistent — each prompt generated a new isolated screen rather than updating the existing design cohesively. No editing tools.

UI Generation Promising
Google AI Studio

The most powerful of the three — but all screens weren't consistent and it couldn't retain context across prompts. Every change risked breaking previous decisions.

Research Strong
ChatGPT + Consensus

The clear winners for structured research and content work — deep conversation, evidence-backed insights, and strong prompt-to-IA workflows. Less capable at converting ideas to visual form.

Outcome

The full case study

The final deliverable documents the complete AI-assisted UX workflow — from defining the problem with ChatGPT to exploring research with Consensus, brainstorming with IdeaMap, and generating UI concepts with three different tools.

Key Learnings

01

AI excels at research, not design execution. Tools like ChatGPT and Consensus were genuinely powerful for synthesising insights. None of the UI generation tools could replace a designer's judgment on consistency and system thinking.

02

Prompting is a design skill. The quality of output was directly tied to how precisely the prompt described the IA, design system, and user mental model. Vague prompts produce vague results.

03

AI tools lack memory and coherence. Every tool struggled to maintain design decisions across multiple prompts — creating screens that didn't feel like one system.
